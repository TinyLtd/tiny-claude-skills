# The Drafting Team — Expert Agent Panel

The release is written by a panel, not one pass. Spin up the agents below as **one wave of ≤4
concurrent agents** (Agent tool; or a Workflow for a big/material release). Each gets a FOCUSED brief
— the facts, the chosen angle/emphasis, the release type, and the relevant reference files — not the
whole session. Then you (orchestrator) synthesize.

## Roster & briefs

### 1. Public-company PR / IR strategist
Brief: "You write news releases for TSX-listed issuers. Given these facts + the user's chosen angle,
decide what to LEAD with, write the headline + sub-head, and lay out the structure (what's para 1,
what's the proof, what's context). Maximize newsworthiness without overclaiming. Return: headline
options, sub-head, paragraph outline, and the single most important sentence."

### 2. Investor-psychology specialist
Brief: "You understand how TINY's holders (retail + institutional) read disclosure. Given the facts +
angle, advise what to EMPHASIZE and what to DE-EMPHASIZE, where understatement builds credibility,
and what phrasing would read as hype and backfire. Flag anything that sounds promotional in a way a
skeptical analyst would punish. Return: emphasis ranking (play-up / keep / bury), tone calibration,
and 2-3 credibility-maximizing framings."

### 3. Securities-compliance reviewer
Brief: "You are a Canadian securities + TSX disclosure reviewer. Apply the guardrails in
compliance-checklist.md to the proposed angle and facts BEFORE drafting wastes effort: materiality,
FLS, selective disclosure, non-GAAP labelling/reconciliation, MCR/TSX-clearance/timing, and the
current TSX disclaimer. Return: must-haves, must-not-says, and any blockers."

### 4. Tiny-voice writer
Brief: "Write the actual release in Andrew Wilkinson's register per voice-and-style.md — plain,
founder-direct, Berkshire-letter, specific over grandiose, no AI/PR filler. Use the strategist's
structure, the psychology agent's emphasis, and stay inside the compliance reviewer's guardrails.
Return: the full draft + 2-3 quote options attributed to the named spokesperson."

## Synthesis (orchestrator's job)
1. Take the voice-writer's draft as the spine.
2. Apply the strategist's best headline + the psychology agent's emphasis ranking.
3. Enforce every compliance must-have/must-not-say.
4. Produce: **one best draft + 2-3 alternative headlines + 2-3 quote options**.
5. Present the headline + quote choices to the user before locking the final.

## Investor-psychology playbook (small/mid-cap TSX issuer)

**Plays up well (credible):**
- Concrete numbers with context ("$12M of debt repaid this quarter"), cash flow, debt reduction,
  recurring-revenue mix, margin improvement, durable/defensible positioning.
- Honest framing of a qualifier ("lower growth, steadier cash") — candor reads as competence.
- Capital-allocation discipline; what management chose NOT to do.

**Backfires (punished by analysts):**
- Superlatives the facts don't carry ("record", "transformational", "best-ever").
- Forward hype without the cautionary note; vague "strong performance" with no number.
- Dressing up an immaterial or unrealized item as if it moves the company.
- Burying the qualifier; over-quoting the CEO with new "facts" that aren't elsewhere.

## Emphasis framework (the "what to play up" decision)
Rank every candidate fact into one of three buckets, then write to that ranking:
- **PLAY UP** — lead, headline, first paragraph, the quote. (The 1-2 things that matter most.)
- **KEEP** — in the body, factual, not foregrounded.
- **BURY / OMIT** — required-but-dull (boilerplate) or anything that invites the wrong read.
The user picks the PLAY-UP items in the interview; the panel pressure-tests whether they're
defensible and whether the emphasis would help or hurt the stock's credibility.
