# Tiny / Andrew Wilkinson — Press-Release Voice

The voice is **founder-direct, plain, and confident** — closer to a Berkshire Hathaway shareholder
letter than a typical small-cap wire release. Promotional but never breathless. Specific beats grand.
Securities rules always override voice.

## Do
- Lead with the news in the first sentence. No throat-clearing.
- Use concrete numbers and plain nouns ("$10.4M of free cash flow", "we bought 70% of Serato"), not
  abstractions ("strong financial performance", "strategic synergies").
- Short sentences. Then a longer one when it earns it. Vary the rhythm.
- Let the CEO quote sound like a person, not a press office — an actual opinion or a candid framing.
- State the bull case in facts and let the reader conclude it's good. Show, don't boast.
- Acknowledge the qualifier honestly when there is one; it builds credibility.

## Don't (kill on sight)
- AI/PR filler: "delve", "landscape", "tapestry", "underscore", "robust", "leverage" (as a verb),
  "pleased to announce" (use sparingly, and only the CEO is "pleased"), "seamless", "best-in-class",
  "game-changing", "poised to".
- Rule-of-three padding ("innovative, scalable, and dynamic").
- Hype that outruns the facts. No "record" / "transformational" / "unprecedented" unless literally
  true and substantiable in the same release.
- Em-dash overuse (max ~1 per paragraph).
- Forward-looking claims without the cautionary note.

## Structure of a Tiny quote (CEO/Chair)
A good quote does one of: frames *why* the news matters in plain language, gives candid context, or
sets direction — without introducing a new material fact that isn't elsewhere in the release.

Good: "We've spent two years making MetaLab more durable, not just bigger. This quarter is what that
looks like — lower growth, steadier cash." — Andrew Wilkinson, Chair

Bad: "We are thrilled to announce record-breaking results that position Tiny as the undisputed leader
in our space."

## Headline conventions
- Factual and specific: "Tiny Reports Q2 2026 Results: Revenue of $X Million, Adjusted EBITDA of $Y
  Million" or "Tiny Acquires 70% of Serato for US$Z Million".
- Optional sub-head adds the one extra fact that matters.
- Avoid adjectives in the headline. The number is the adjective.

## Promotional-but-not-overly calibration
Think: a smart, skeptical investor should finish the release feeling the news is genuinely good
*because of the facts*, not because they were told to feel that way. If a sentence would make that
investor roll their eyes, cut it.
