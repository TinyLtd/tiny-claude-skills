# Virtual AGM — Change Notice & Notice-of-Change Resolution

Templates for shifting a scheduled in-person AGM to virtual-only, matching the company's
actual house format. Two artifacts: (1) the directors' resolution authorizing the change,
and (2) the shareholder-facing Notice of Change. The resolution follows the boilerplate in
the corp-governance skill's `resolution-templates.md`; the recital block and the notice
content are below.

## 1. Notice-of-change directors' resolution — recital + operative block

```
ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING

WHEREAS, on [DATE], the Board approved the holding of the annual general meeting of the
shareholders (the "Meeting") as an in-person meeting to be held at [VENUE / ADDRESS];

AND WHEREAS, the Company proposes to change the Meeting to a virtual-only format, in order to
provide the Company's shareholders with an equal opportunity to attend and participate in the
Meeting regardless of geographical location;

AND WHEREAS, in connection with the change of format of the Meeting, the Company will issue a
notice of change (the "Notice of Change") such Notice of Change to be mailed to the
shareholders of the Company;

AND WHEREAS, the Board has determined that it is advisable and in the best interest of the
Company to authorize and direct the Company to: (i) change the Meeting to a virtual-only
format in accordance with the Notice of Change; and (ii) proceed with the mailing of the
Notice of Change to the shareholders of the Company.

NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED THAT:

1. The change of the Meeting from an in-person meeting to a virtual-only meeting in accordance
   with the Notice of Change be and is hereby ratified, authorized and approved.

2. The Notice of Change, substantially in the form provided to the Board, be and is hereby
   authorized and approved, and any one director or officer of the Company (an "Authorized
   Signatory") be and is hereby authorized and directed for and on behalf of the Company to
   amend such document as such person may negotiate and approve, in their sole discretion.
```

Follow with the long-form GENERAL AUTHORIZATION clause and a counterparts variant from
`resolution-templates.md`, then the signature block. The notice-of-change resolution in the
examples used **Variant A** counterparts and a separate footer signature page titled
`[Company] – Signature Page to Authorizing Resolutions re: Notice of Change`.

## 2. Shareholder-facing Notice of Change — structure

```
IMPORTANT NOTICE
[COMPANY]'S ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING OF SHAREHOLDERS TO BE HELD ON [DATE], AT [TIME]
([TIMEZONE]), WILL BE HELD IN A VIRTUAL-ONLY MEETING FORMAT. YOU WILL NOT BE ABLE TO ATTEND
THE MEETING PHYSICALLY.

[Opening paragraph: streamline attendance/voting; equal opportunity regardless of geography;
"There have been no other changes to the Meeting."]

The Meeting can be accessed by logging in through the following link: [meetnow.global/XXXXX].

Registered Shareholders as of [RECORD DATE/TIME] and duly appointed proxyholders will be able
to attend, participate, ask questions and vote. Beneficial (non-registered) holders who have
not appointed themselves may attend as guests only (no vote, no questions).

HOW TO PARTICIPATE
- Registered Shareholders: 15-digit control number (on the form of proxy or email notification).
- Duly Appointed Proxyholders: Computershare provides an Invite Code after the voting deadline.

APPOINTING A PROXYHOLDER
Submit proxy / voting instruction form first, THEN register the proxyholder at
[computershare.com/...] by [TIME] on [DEADLINE DATE] so Computershare can email an Invite Code.
Failure to register = proxyholder gets no Invite Code.

VOTING AT THE MEETING
- Proxy deposit deadline: [TIME] on [DATE] (or 48 hours, excluding weekends/holidays, before
  any adjourned/postponed meeting).
- A shareholder who submitted a proxy but then votes by ballot at the webcast: the ballot
  counts and the proxy is disregarded.
- US beneficial holders: obtain a legal proxy from broker/bank, then register with
  Computershare (uslegalproxy@computershare.com) labelled "Legal Proxy" by the deadline.

Votes and proxies are counted, verified and tabulated by Computershare. Meeting materials are
on the Company's website and on SEDAR+ (sedarplus.ca).
```

## Key mechanics to track
- **Voting/proxy deadline**: typically 11:00 a.m. (Pacific) two business days before the
  meeting; example used [meeting June 9] → deadline June 5.
- **Proxyholder registration** is a *separate* step from proxy submission — flag this on every
  reminder; it's the most common shareholder error.
- **Platform**: Computershare Meeting (meetnow.global) with 15-digit control numbers + Invite
  Codes.
- The notice states **"There have been no other changes to the Meeting"** — keep that line; it
  reassures that only the format changed.
