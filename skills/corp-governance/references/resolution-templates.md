# Resolution Templates — Exact House Boilerplate

These are the verbatim clauses used in the company's actual directors' and committee
resolutions. Match this language exactly. Fill bracketed placeholders. Use the legal
entity name **exactly as it appears on the Certificate of Incumbency** (in the examples,
`TINY LTD.` — all caps in title blocks). Do not substitute a trade name.

Pull the current director roster from the most recent **Certificate of Incumbency** or the
last signed resolution. Names appear in **ALL CAPS** in signature blocks.

> ⚠️ Validity note: a directors'/committee resolution in writing is effective only if signed by
> **ALL** directors/members entitled to vote on it — a majority is not enough (CBCA s. 117(1); OBCA
> s. 129; BCBCA s. 140). The "constituting all of the members" recital reflects this; confirm every
> entitled director has signed before treating the resolution as passed.

## 1. Directors' resolution — standard skeleton

```
[COMPANY NAME]
DIRECTORS RESOLUTION

The undersigned, constituting all of the members of the board of directors (the "Board")
of [COMPANY NAME] (the "Company"), acting pursuant to the authority conferred upon them
under the Canada Business Corporations Act, do hereby consent to and adopt in writing the
following resolutions as of [DATE], passed without meeting, pursuant to which, under the
Company's By-Laws, will be deemed to have the same force and effect as if passed at a
meeting of the Board properly called and constituted for the transaction of business.

[ALL-CAPS HEADING FOR THE MATTER]

WHEREAS [recital of background fact];

AND WHEREAS [further recital];

AND WHEREAS the [Committee, if applicable] has reviewed and recommended Board approval of
[the matter];

AND WHEREAS the Board has reviewed [the matter] and considers it to be in the best
interests of the Company to approve [the matter].

NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED THAT:

1. [Operative resolution — state precisely what is approved, "subject to" any conditions
   such as shareholder approval or Exchange acceptance.]

2. [Further operative paragraph if needed.]

[GENERAL AUTHORIZATION — see clause 3 below]

[COUNTERPARTS — see clause 4, pick the variant in use]

[DATING LINE — see clause 5]

[SIGNATURE BLOCK — see clause 6]
```

Note: some resolutions use **`IT IS RESOLVED that`** (lower-case operative verb) instead of
the numbered `NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED THAT:` form — both are in use. The minutes-approval
resolution uses the `WHEREAS … IT IS RESOLVED that …` short form.

## 2. Committee resolution — preamble variant

For a Compensation, Governance and Nominating (CG&N) Committee resolution, swap the preamble:

```
[COMPANY NAME]
COMPENSATION, GOVERNANCE AND NOMINATING COMMITTEE RESOLUTION

The undersigned, constituting all of the members of the Compensation, Governance and
Nominating Committee (the "CG&N Committee") of [COMPANY NAME] (the "Company"), acting
pursuant to the authority conferred upon them under the Company's by-laws and the CG&N
Committee charter, do hereby consent to and adopt in writing the following resolutions,
passed without meeting, pursuant to which, will be deemed to have the same force and effect
as if passed at a meeting of the CG&N Committee properly called and constituted for the
transaction of business.
```

A committee that recommends (rather than approves) ends with:
`NOW THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED THAT the [Committee] hereby recommends that the Board of Directors approve [the matter].`

## 3. General authorization clause (two variants in use)

**Long form** (used on substantive resolutions, e.g. equity plan, notice of change):

```
GENERAL AUTHORIZATION

BE IT RESOLVED THAT all acts and things previously done and performed (or caused to be done
and performed) in connection with or in furtherance of the matters contemplated by these
resolutions are hereby ratified, confirmed, and approved. Except to the extent that certain
documents referred to above are to be executed by specified officers of the Company, any one
director or officer of the Company is hereby authorized in the name and on behalf of the
Company to do and perform all such further acts and things including, but not limited to,
executing and delivering, and where necessary or appropriate, filing with the appropriate
regulatory authorities, all such notices, certificates, contracts, bonds, agreements,
documents, instruments, receipts, or other papers, including, and making all such payments,
including payments of all fees and expenses, as in the judgment of such officer or director
shall be necessary, desirable or appropriate to complete each of the foregoing resolutions
adopted by the Board and each of the transactions contemplated thereby.
```

**Short form** (used on minutes-approval and lighter resolutions, headed `GENERAL`):

```
GENERAL

Any director or officer of the Company is hereby authorized to execute, for and in the name
of and on behalf of the Company, under the corporate seal of the Company or otherwise, all
such other agreements, documents, certificates, filings and undertakings as such officer or
director may consider necessary or desirable to give effect to the foregoing resolutions and
perform and do all such other acts and things as such officer or director in his or her sole
discretion (including without limitation all necessary filings under applicable securities
and other laws) may consider to be necessary, desirable or useful for the purposes of giving
effect to any of the foregoing resolutions. All such executions, acts and things heretofore
or hereafter done by such directors or officers, or any one or more of them acting alone, in
connection with and in furtherance of the intent and purposes of the foregoing resolutions
be, and hereby are, approved, ratified and confirmed in all respects as the act and deed of
the Company.
```

## 4. Counterparts / electronic-execution clause (THREE variants — match the one in use)

**Variant A — "one or more counterparts"** (equity plan, notice of change):
```
This resolution may be signed by the directors in one or more counterparts, no one of which
must be signed by all of the directors but all of which together shall have the same force
and effect as if the directors had signed the same document. An electronically signed copy
of this document shall constitute sufficient evidence of the execution of the resolutions.
```

**Variant B — "single instrument" (names DocuSign explicitly)** (committee, minutes approval):
```
COUNTERPARTS

These resolutions may be signed in counterpart and such counterparts together shall
constitute a single instrument. Delivery of an executed counterpart of these resolutions by
electronic means, including by facsimile transmission, by electronic delivery in portable
document format (".pdf") or DocuSign, shall be equally effective as delivery of a manually
executed counterpart hereof. Notwithstanding the date of execution of these resolutions by
the undersigned, these resolutions shall be deemed to be executed as of the date set forth
below.
```

**Variant C — inline numbered paragraph** (AGM meeting-materials resolution):
```
This resolution may be executed in separate counterparts, and all such executed counterparts
when taken together shall constitute one (1) resolution. The Company shall be entitled to
rely on delivery of a facsimile or email copy of the executed resolution and such copy shall
be legally effective to create a valid and binding resolution.
```

## 5. Dating line (three forms in use)

- `DATED effective as of the first date written above` (when the date is in the preamble)
- `Dated as of the [Xth] day of [Month], [Year].`
- `Dated this [Xth] day of [Month], [Year].`

## 6. Signature block (two-column, ALL CAPS names)

```
______________________________________________________________
[DIRECTOR 1 — ALL CAPS]                    [DIRECTOR 2 — ALL CAPS]


______________________________________________________________
[DIRECTOR 3 — ALL CAPS]                    [DIRECTOR 4 — ALL CAPS]


_______________________________
[DIRECTOR 5 — ALL CAPS]
```

For executed DocuSign output the format is the DocuSign signature line over the printed name:
```
DocuSigned by: [signature]          Signed by: [signature]
[DIRECTOR 1]                        [DIRECTOR 2]
```

Some resolutions carry a footer label, e.g. `[Company] – Signature Page to Authorizing Resolutions re: [Matter]`.
